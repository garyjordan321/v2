What Is Pump Dot Fun, What Is The Hyped Mascot Meta, How Does Satoshi Tie Into It All?

by
Vivian Nguyen
Oct. 29, 2024

Pump.fun is a Solana-based platform shaking up the meme coin world by letting anyone create tokens in minutes without coding skills. Launched in January 2024, its gained massive traction with over 50,000 users and nearly two million tokens created. The platforms fair launch modelno pre-sales or insider perksensures a level playing field, while its bonding curve system encourages early investment and boosts token value as demand grows. Though most tokens see little success, a few, like PNUT with a multi-billion dollar market cap, have soared. With low fees, instant liquidity, and a community-driven vibe, Pump.fun has made meme coins more accessible than ever.

The Pump Fun market follows varying trends, sometimes switching up within a couple of hours. Yet one meta (trend) that has persistently remained at the forefront of the game is the Mascot trend. Since the Vaticans announcement of their Mascot Of The Holy Year, Luce over a month ago, some of the top performing tokens launched from pumpfun have been Mascot-related. Luce had an original market cap of 5k and now boastfully sits at a market cap of 230 million. Another mascot coin is Lester, the mascot of Litecoin, and is hovering close to a 100 million dollar market cap. So with this in mind, our team here at Crypto Briefing decided to do some digging and attempt to uncover a mascot of the number 1 thing crypto evolves around Bitcoin.

After some extensive research our team discovered the 'P2Pfoundation' forum where Satoshi Nakamoto initiated his first public discussion about bitcoin. Mainly detailing what the blockchain is and Bitcoins purpose, a user by the name of 'Joerg Baac' on February 17, 2009 responds to a comment Satoshi made stating "An additional side note, i think you should brand bitcoin with a white cat. reason being, well, because banks have historically associated themselves with pigs. Whats the opposite of a dirty pig? I hygienic pure white cat. Sorry, but some more light hearted food for thought." Our inference of this statement made to Satoshi is that an individual in contact with Bitcoin's founder during its early years suggested that the cryptocurrency should have a white cat Mascot. 16 years later it seems this proposal was neglected but with this information surfacing once again its anyones guess at the future.
